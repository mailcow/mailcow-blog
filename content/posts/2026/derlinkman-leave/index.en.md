**Moohoo everyone.**

Today I want to officially announce <u>my</u> departure from The Infrastructure Company GmbH and thus from the mailcow team as well. Effective May 1st, 2026, I will no longer be part of the team.

The past years have been a formative time for me both professionally and personally. Together with the community, contributors, and users, we were able to continuously develop and stabilize mailcow into the solution it is today. The countless deployments, discussions, issues, pull requests, and critical feedback have made the project even stronger.

Special thanks go to the people in the forum and Telegram group who help other mailcow users **for free** in their spare time. This commitment is not taken for granted. The community thrives on exactly this dedication, helpfulness, knowledge sharing, and technical passion. You are a central part of what makes mailcow special.

I also want to thank everyone who has supported mailcow financially, whether through the mailcow Stay-Awesome-License, or in other ways. This support is instrumental in ensuring infrastructure, development, and long-term stability of the project. This is not a given and deserves explicit recognition.

I also want to thank everyone who uses, tests, documents, reports bugs, or actively contributes to the development of mailcow. My gratitude also goes to those who simply use mailcow productively and recommend it. Every installation, every recommendation, and every positive feedback contributes to the project's growth and visibility. Your support, trust, and constructive collaboration have sustained mailcow over the years.

{{< admonition type=info title="Important" open=true >}}

My departure does **not** mean that mailcow is being discontinued or ending in any way.

The project continues normally. Development continues, the team remains active, and mailcow remains a stable and future-proof solution.

{{< /admonition >}}

A new chapter begins for me now. I take with me much experience, many encounters, and many technical challenges that have shaped me profoundly.

This is my personal farewell from the project, but not a farewell from mailcow itself. **mailcow will continue**, evolve, and will continue to be supported by a dedicated team and strong community. I wish the project continued stability, innovation, and the success it deserves.

Thank you for the wonderful time, the collaboration, and the trust.

It was an honor to be part of this project.

**Until then, happy mailing and stay healthy!**

Your DerLinkman aka. Niklas
